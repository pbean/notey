## Successes

1. **RISK-006 (CLI injection, score 6) was genuinely mitigated, not just claimed.** Injection / path-traversal / control-char payloads are stored **literally** (parameterized queries throughout); content is capped at 1 MiB **before** the buffer fills (6.3 caught `read_to_end` buffering the whole pipe first); and — easily missed — **6.4/6.5 sanitize control bytes in CLI stdout** to prevent terminal-escape injection on the *output* side.

2. **The "don't fork persistence" rule held perfectly.** The IPC server delegates to the same `services::notes / search_service / workspace_service` functions. Proof: `acl_tests.rs::EXPECTED_COMMANDS` is unchanged — **no new Tauri commands** were added, so the permission-TOML gotcha (Epic 5 Action Item 4) **didn't even recur** this epic.

3. **Strong socket-security review.** Adversarial review caught and fixed: non-atomic create-across-workspace-resolution (wrapped in one transaction), non-user-scoped fallback socket names, reclaiming *non-socket* paths during stale-reclaim, and — the best instinct — **aborting startup if `0600` cannot be applied** rather than serving an unprotected endpoint.

4. **The escalation seam proved itself.** Story 6.7's "a timed-out `add` may have already committed server-side" was **not auto-guessed** — it escalated to a human via `bmad-auto-resolve`, landing a real decision: at-least-once semantics, report the mutating timeout as **indeterminate** ("your note may or may not have been saved; run `notey list` to check"), never claim failure, never auto-retry. Exactly-once explicitly out of scope.

5. **The frozen-spec contract is now a mature review engine.** Ledgers are full of dismissals citing it — name non-uniqueness is *required*, `..` rejection is *intentional*, literal `<mark>` stripping is *by design*. It keeps the reviewer maximally cynical on implementation while preventing wasted cycles re-arguing intent.

## Challenges

1. **The singleflight helper was never built — and a 7th hand-rolled guard was added.** Epic 5's Action Item 2 (extract a shared singleflight helper, replace the 6 hand-rolled in-flight guards) sat in `backlog`. Story 6.6 then added guard **#7** (`refreshInFlight` in `realtimeSync.ts:16`). The flagged debt grew while its own fix waited.

2. **Still zero new E2E — six epics running.** Story 6.6 explicitly defers live verification of the socket→event→desktop-refresh path to a *"Nightly 6.E2E-001"* that does not exist. The only E2E suite remains the 8 Epic-1 capture-loop tests (`e2e/run.mjs`). Unit/integration coverage is strong (94 CLI + 43 new backend), but the **live seam between the two processes** is verified by hand only — on the most integration-shaped epic, driven unattended by bmad-auto.

3. **"CLI stories" quietly became backend-contract stories.** 6.4 and 6.5 each required a *server-side* change — switching the `list_notes` / `search_notes` IPC payload from `workspaceId` to `workspaceName` and adding name-enrichment + name-filtering to the services. Reasonable, but the "CLI" framing understated the cross-boundary work.

4. **Retro-tracking drift — third retro in a row.** Epic 5 Action Items 1, 2, 4 still read `backlog` in `sprint-status.yaml` even though Item 1's test-*design* shipped and Item 4 was moot this epic. Same close-out-discipline gap named at Epic 4 and Epic 5.

5. **High dismiss-to-patch ratio leaning on "out of scope."** Story 6.7 alone carried ~16 dismissals, several "previously dismissed in the ledger." Some are genuine future hardening — broken-pipe panic on output, orphaned timeout worker threads, response-size cap, fuller connect-error taxonomy. Only one reached the DW ledger (DW-85). Risk: "dismissed as out-of-scope" becoming `/dev/null` instead of `deferred-work.md`.

---

## Key Insights

1. **"Before the next epic" is a wish, not a gate.** Item 1 was marked CRITICAL and "gates Epic 6" — yet Epic 6 ran to completion without it closing. A commitment that doesn't mechanically block isn't a gate. The fix is not "re-flag it"; it is either a real gate or a conscious, owned acceptance of the risk. (This retro: Pinkyd took direct ownership of both carried items, deliberately off the gate question.)

2. **Deferred refactors compete with features and lose — and can actively grow.** The cross-cutting concurrency-with-feedback abstraction is now two retros old and added a 7th consumer. High-value refactors only happen when scheduled as their own work, not appended to "prep."

3. **The E2E gap is now structurally dangerous, and Epic 7 is where it bites.** bmad-auto drives whole epics unattended, we just crossed a process boundary, and Epic 7 (focus traps, tab order, focus-ring contrast, `prefers-reduced-motion`, WCAG AA audit) is the most E2E-shaped epic yet. The first feature E2E pays off most precisely now.

4. **The human-escalation loop is a proven capability.** `bmad-auto-resolve` resolving the at-least-once question shows the seam works for true ambiguities — not just confident guesses. Worth leaning on for the next genuine fork.

5. **Cross-boundary epics need paired client+server scoping.** "CLI story" was a misnomer once the contract crossed the socket. Future transport-spanning epics should plan server and client as one scope from the start.
